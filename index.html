CelluWar

The Rules

CelluWar is a game based on Conway's Game of Life. To play CelluWar, you must learn the 4 rules of "Game of Life." The Game of Life is a grid of squares which contains dead or alive cells. These cells operate according to a set of rules which govern the birth and death of individual cells.

Conway's "Game of Life" has 4 rules:

  1. Cells that have exactly 2 or 3 neighbors survive.
  2. Cells with fewer than 2 neighbors die due to under-population.
  3. Cells with more than 3 neighbors die due to over-population.
  4. Empty locations (black) that have exactly 3 neighbors reproduce a daughter cell.

CelluWar is a two-player extension of Game of Life. You will play as the red cells, and the computer will play as the blue cells. Red and blue cells are born and die according the rules of Conway's Game of Life, with one exception: each player is allowed to place one cell on the board during each round. The game play is as follows:

  1. Your move: select a square to turn red.
  2. Computer's move: computer selects a square to turn blue.
  3. Conway's move: Conway's Game of Life is simulated (1 step).

Your objective is to remove all the blue cells from the board while at least one red cell remains alive. The game can only end after the Game of Life is Simulated.

Enable Hints

The rules can be a bit confusing. Toggle the switch on and off, to show hints. All squares that will be dead (empty) are marked with an "x" while squares that will be alive are marked with a "✓" in the corresponding color ("✓" or "✓").
